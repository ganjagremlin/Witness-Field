Why is higher elevation colder temperature? For example, why is mount Everest cold rather than warm given its closest to the sun.


ChatGPT said:
The temperature at higher elevations, like on Mount Everest, is colder despite being closer to the sun because of how Earth's atmosphere works:

Atmospheric Pressure Decreases with Altitude:
As you ascend, the atmospheric pressure decreases, meaning there are fewer air molecules. These air molecules are essential for retaining and transferring heat. With less air, there is less ability for the atmosphere to trap heat, making it colder.

Heat Absorption Happens at Earth's Surface:
Most of the Earth's warmth comes from the surface absorbing sunlight and radiating heat back into the atmosphere. At higher altitudes, you are farther away from this heat source. The air at elevation doesn’t have the dense atmosphere below to warm it up.

Lower Heat Retention:
At higher altitudes, the thin air cannot hold heat effectively. This means any heat that is present quickly dissipates into space rather than staying near the surface.

Adiabatic Cooling:
As air rises, it expands because of the lower pressure. This expansion causes the air to cool. This phenomenon, called adiabatic cooling, contributes to the colder temperatures at high altitudes.

Less Greenhouse Effect:
At high elevations, there are fewer greenhouse gases to trap heat. Near sea level, these gases play a significant role in keeping temperatures warmer.

Even though Mount Everest is technically closer to the sun, the difference in distance is negligible compared to the effects of atmospheric conditions.
